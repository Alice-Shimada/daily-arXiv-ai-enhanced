TL;DR: 本文利用银河系测绘器（MWM DR19）的高分辨率近红外光谱，对球状星团ω Centauri中恒星成员进行无先验假设的七维化学丰度空间聚类分析，识别出10个化学上独立的恒星种群，并揭示其源于四种核合成通道；结果支持ω Cen是被银河系吸积的矮星系核残余。

Motivation: ω Centauri作为银河系中质量最大的球状星团，其恒星种群化学复杂性长期缺乏统一表征，亟需大样本、高精度、无偏倚的化学丰度分析以厘清其形成与演化历史。

Method: 基于MWM DR19高分辨率近红外光谱，构建迄今最大、最均匀的ω Cen成员星化学丰度样本；在七维化学丰度空间中应用Ward-linkage层次聚类，不预设种群数量或边界，识别化学种群并分析其核合成特征与演化时标。

Result: 发现10个化学上显著区分的恒星种群，对应铁峰、α元素、CNO循环及高温质子俘获四类核合成通道；呈现两组主导结构，反映超新星与AGB星的不同增丰路径；s-过程丰度与铁峰元素解耦上升、Ia型超新星贡献微弱、存在原初成分中间金属丰度种群、最富金属种群可能形成于并入银河系晕之后；所有种群均位于吸积起源特征的[Al/Fe]–[Mg/Mn]平面区域。

Conclusion: ω Centauri并非典型球状星团，而是被银河系吸积的矮星系核遗迹；其多代恒星形成与复杂化学增丰由内部（AGB主导轻元素与s-过程）和外部（并合事件）过程共同驱动；该工作为后续化学动力学联合研究提供了关键框架。

Abstract: $ω$~Centauri, the most massive globular cluster in the Milky Way, exhibits a level of stellar population complexity that has long resisted a unified chemical characterisation. We exploit high-resolution near-infrared spectroscopy from the Milky Way Mapper survey (MWM DR19) to construct one of the largest homogeneously analysed samples of $ω$~Cen members to date. Applying Ward-linkage hierarchical clustering in a seven-dimensional chemical abundance space, without prior assumptions on population number or boundaries, we identify ten chemically distinct stellar populations. Their nucleosynthetic signatures trace four enrichment channels: iron-peak, $α$-element, CNO-cycle, and high-temperature proton-capture processes. The populations organise into two dominant groups separated by a large light-element spread at a modest iron baseline, consistent with AGB-driven self-enrichment. This dichotomy reflects distinct enrichment pathways: core-collapse supernovae establish the iron baseline, while AGB stars dominate light-element and $s$-process enrichment. A decoupled rise in $s$-process abundances relative to iron-peak elements, together with sub-dominant Type~Ia contributions across all metallicities, indicates evolution on timescales shorter than the characteristic Type~Ia delay time. One intermediate-metallicity population retains a primordial composition, providing evidence for spatially segregated enrichment within the progenitor. The most metal-rich component may trace star formation continuing after accretion into the Milky Way halo. All populations lie in the accreted regime of the $[\mathrm{Al/Fe}]$--$[\mathrm{Mg/Mn}]$ plane, supporting an ex-situ origin. These results reinforce the interpretation of $ω$~Cen as the remnant nucleus of an accreted dwarf galaxy and provide a framework for future chemo-dynamical studies.
