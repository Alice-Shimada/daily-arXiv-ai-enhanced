### [1] [Gravitational dressing: from the crossed product to more general algebraic and mathematical structure](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.24833)
*Steven B. Giddings*

Main category: hep-th

TL;DR: 通过扩展标准引力 dressing 构造，本文恢复了交叉积以及从III型到II型冯诺依曼代数的转变，揭示了量子引力中更普遍代数结构的一角。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 探索量子引力中的代数结构，特别是冯诺依曼代数类型的转变如何与引力现象相关联。

Method: 扩展已有的标准dressing构造，用于定义引力分裂的微扰理论，并研究信息局部化的近似方法。

Result: 成功恢复了交叉积及冯诺依曼代数从III型到II型的转变；发现了分离区域间的非对易性，并在非微扰层次上与引力的全息行为可能解释相联系。

Conclusion: 该研究表明，代数类型的变化是量子引力中更广泛数学结构的一部分，暗示了非对易几何和全息原理在理解引力本质方面的重要性。

Abstract: The crossed product, and consequent transition from von Neumann algebras of
type III to II, is recovered from a truncation of more general gravitational
dressing constructions, about certain spacetimes. This is done by extending
"standard dressing" constructions previously used to give a perturbative
definition of "gravitational splittings," defining approximate localization of
information. This result appears to illustrate that this algebraic transition
is a small piece of a more general algebraic, or other mathematical, structure
associated with quantum gravity. The leading-order structure involves
noncommutativity from separated regions, and at the nonperturbative level
connects with a possible explanation of holographic behavior for gravity.

### [2] [A Precise $α_s$ Determination from the R-improved QCD Static Energy](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.24846)
*Jose M. Mena-Valle,Vicent Mateu,Pablo G. Ortega*

Main category: hep-ph

TL;DR: 本文通过改进的三环固定阶预测和重正化群演化，结合格点QCD模拟，高精度确定了强耦合常数αs，在Z极点处得到αs(mZ)=0.1166±0.0009，与世界平均值一致。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 精确测定强耦合常数αs对于验证标准模型和探索新物理至关重要，传统方法在长距离上存在理论不确定性，需改进以提高精度。

Method: 采用R改进去除u=1/2 renormalon并求和相关的大对数项，结合依赖半径的重正化尺度（profile functions），将微扰理论的有效性扩展到约0.5 fm的距离，并在N³LL精度下对超软对数进行重正化群求和。

Result: 在Z极点处得到αs(nf=5)(mZ) = 0.1166 ± 0.0009，误差与世界平均值相当，结果一致。

Conclusion: 该方法显著提高了αs的提取精度，验证了微扰QCD在中等距离下的有效性，并为未来更精确的强相互作用研究提供了可靠工具。

Abstract: The strong coupling $\alpha_s$ is determined with high precision from fits to
lattice QCD simulations on the static energy. Our theoretical setup relies on
R-improving the three-loop fixed-order prediction for the static energy by
removing its $u=1/2$ renormalon and summing up the associated large (infrared)
logarithms which, in combination with radius-dependent renormalization scales
(called profile functions) extends the validity of perturbation theory to
distances up to $\sim 0.5\,$fm. Furthermore, we resum large ultrasoft
logarithms to N$^3$LL accuracy using renormalization group evolution. We have
checked that the standard four-loop R-evolution treats N$^4$LL and higher
remnants in a non-symmetric way, hence we also account for this potential bias.
Our estimate of the perturbative uncertainty is based on a random scan over the
parameters specifying the profile functions and the treatment of R-evolution.
We also devise a method to statistically combine into a single dataset results
from independent simulations which use different lattice spacing and cover
various ranges, which can be used to carry out fits in a much faster way. We
explore the dependence of the extracted $\alpha_s$ value on the smallest and
largest distances included in the dataset, on how R-evolution is treated, on
how the fit is performed, and on the accuracy of ultrasoft resummation. From
our final analysis, after evolving to the $Z$-pole we obtain
$\alpha^{(n_f=5)}_s(m_Z)=0.1166\pm 0.0009$, compatible with the world average
with similar incertitude.

</details>
